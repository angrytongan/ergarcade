Single user setup

%youtube-placehold-ABd-dokXh_4^/images/placeholder-640x360.jpg%

The most common setup will be when you are training on your own. You'll need:

  • A %concept2% indoor rower, SkiErg or BikeErg.
  • A USB A-to-B cable. Also called a USB printer cable, this cord has a flat USB connector at one end and a square-ish connector at the other. Cables should be no longer than 5 metres in length.
  • A computer running Mac OS X. This can be a desktop or laptop computer and must have a USB port available.

Putting everything together:

  1. Power on your computer.
  2. Plug the flat end of the USB cable into your computer / laptop.
  3. Plug the square-ish end of the USB cable into your %performance-monitor%. This can be found on the underside of the monitor (PM3, PM4) or the rear (PM5).
  4. Download and install the %c2proxy% application on your computer.

Once %c2proxy% has been installed, it will appear in the system tray of your computer. Clicking on the %c2proxy% icon will show your connected ergometer. Click on Launch browser to bring you to the %ergarcade% website where you can choose from a range of %tools%, %visualisations% or %games% to play!

Didn't work? Check the %troubleshooting% section for help.

Multi-user setup

%youtube-placehold-ABd-dokXh_4^/images/placeholder-640x360.jpg%

Multi-user setups are for class or gym environments, when you want to have a birds' eye view across all athletes at the same time. You'll need:

  • Multiple %concept2% indoor rowers, SkiErgs or BikeErgs. You can mix and match rowers, SkiErgs and BikeErgs.
  • One USB A-to-B cable per ergometer. Also called a USB printer cable, this cord has a flat USB connector at one end and a square-ish connector at the other. Cables should be no longer than 5 metres in length.
  • A powered USB hub. A USB hub has multiple USB ports, allowing you to plug in multiple cables that go to your ergometers and only take up one USB port on your computer. The USB hub should be powered; non-powered hubs will not function correctly.
  • A computer running Mac OS X. This can be a desktop or laptop computer and must have a USB port available.

Putting everything together:

  1. Power on your computer.
  2. Plug the flat end of the USB hub cable into your computer / laptop. Turn the power on to the hub.
  3. Plug in one USB cable per port on the USB hub. Plug the other end of each of these cables (the square-ish connector) to each ergometer %performance-monitor%; these can be found on the underside of the monitor (PM4) or the rear (PM5).
  4. Download and install the %c2proxy% application on your computer.

Once %c2proxy% has been installed, it will appear in the system tray of your computer. Clicking on the %c2proxy% icon will show your connected ergometers. Click on Launch browser to bring you to the %ergarcade% website where you can choose from a range of %tools%, %visualisations% or %games% to play!

Didn't work? Check the %troubleshooting% section for help.

Remote Viewing

%youtube-placehold-ABd-dokXh_4^/images/placeholder-640x360.jpg%

Remote viewing allows devices other than your computer to run %ergarcade% applications and gather ergometer data from your computer. An example would be a coaching situation where you have a multi-user setup and are using %omniscient% on a tablet or phone to watch all your athlete's force curves while moving around and critiquing form.

Once you have your environment setup (either single or multi):

  1. Click on the %c2proxy% icon in the system tray of your computer, and open the Interfaces sub-menu. A list of IP addresses will appear; these are the interface addresses that exist on your computer.

Casting

Casting is the ability for a computer to send it's video output to another screen, such as a television or smart monitor. This can be very useful in a class environment, allowing all athletes to see performance simultaneously or to play some multi-user %visualisations% or %games%.

While casting is not built-into %ergarcade%, you can absolutely install casting software on your computer and have your screen sent to another device.

Consult the documentation that came with your casting hardware or software.
